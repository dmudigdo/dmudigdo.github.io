One thing that intrigued me about recent reviews of generative AI products is that they promise some amazing result 'in minutes'. With the right prompting, you can have AI create a website 'in minutes' or a slideshow presentation 'in minutes'. In one of the video reviews for Google's Notebook LM, someone promised the creation of a talk show 'in minutes'. That's right, given some inputs for a chosen topic, Notebook LM could generate a talk show on that topic. In minutes. Now this I had to see(hear).

## Notebook LM: A First Glance
[Notebook LM](https://notebooklm.google/){:target="_blank"} was released in 2023, it was is billed as a learning tool: "Understand  Anything - Your research and thinking partner". So you give it some inputs on a given topic in the form of URLs, PDFs and so on, and NotebookLM can generate summaries of the topic, based on those inputs. The output can be simply a summary in the form of a blog post, but it can also be a video explainer. Or a talk show. This is what intrigued me most out of all the outputs, because the AI would have to generate a conversation and banter between two characters, and make it flow.

## Getting Started
Notebook LM is billed as a learning tool, but to kick the tyres, I decided to test it on a topic I already knew a lot about: me! I chose the inputs to be my websites over the years, some current, some that have been defunct for over a decade:
* [Guangzhou TV interview from 2008](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2vOMpr6R8kg){:target="_blank"}
* [Music website circa 2012](https://musiclessons.didi.com.au){:target="_blank"}
* [Music lessons website circa 2020](https://didi.com.au/pre2012/){:target="_blank"}
* [Current LinkedIn profile](https://www.linkedin.com/in/didimudigdo/){:target="_blank"}
* [This blog]({% link about.md %}).

For good measure, I also added a website that included a significant musical achievement: being in the top 10 (jazz category) in a [2005 songwriting contest](https://www.asai.org.au/2005-contest/){:target="_blank"}.

But I went straight to the one that intrigued me the most, the talkshow (NotebookLM calls it 'Deep Dive', under 'Audio'). Now when someone says 'in minutes' I usually interpret this to be under 10 minutes. If something took 11 minutes, I don't believe one can say that it took 'minutes'. As promised, the talk show was generated in about 8 minutes.

## The Results
So what do you think?

I was quite impressed, an AI generated talk show about myself! While billed as a learning tool, it turns out that Notebook LM is quite good for encouraging people who wonder what they have been doing with their lives. I decided to go all out and put it [on SoundCloud](https://soundcloud.com/didi-mudigdo/podcast-didi-mudigdo-anne-irving-adam-ingram){:target="_blank"}, along with a matching tacky thumbnail image. ## Conclusion
What I just did would more appropriately be classed as 'party trick'. However, it does illustrate the capabilities of NotebookLM. The non-learning possible uses are endless, just have a look on YouTube to see what people are doing with it. Some are even using it to [generate monetisable content](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=monetise+notebooklm){:target="_blank"}, e.g. finding book summaries that are in demand, getting NotebookLM to summarise it (text, audio or video), and then sell it. I hope this has got you interested in trying it out, just go to notebooklm.google.com.
